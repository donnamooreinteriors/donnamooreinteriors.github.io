The Home Place

Canyon Creek Cottage, affectionately called The Home Place, belonged to my family for seventy-eight years. A total restoration that lasted almost two years was a labor of love. Memories of extended family reunions, grass-stained knees, homemade peach ice cream and a pet rabbit flooded my mind. I preserved original heart pine floors, bead board walls and ceilings, plastered walls, period doors, windows and hardware. In the large kitchen, I designed a functioning island constructed from an old cast iron sewing machine frame with a wooden top and cabinet base to make it counter height. If kitchens had islands when this simple cottage was built, I imagine that’s how they may have looked. I recognized treasures like my grandma’s coffee percolator that’s pictured in the center of one of these photos and saved heirloom plants including an antique Seven Sisters rosebush. As a child, this is where I created rooms in old baking pans using moss, mirror fragments, stones and other objects in the woods down that steep hill by the creek.
